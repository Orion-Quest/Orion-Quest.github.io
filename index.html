Timeline

I see learning as a route that becomes clearer over time. Each stage is not a finish line, but a launch point for the next one.

01 · Starting Point: Images and Video Learning how to read, display, draw, and process frames to build intuition for visual data.
02 · Seeing Boundaries: Color Space and Segmentation Understanding color representations and using HSV ranges to separate targets from the background.
03 · Clearing Noise: Preprocessing and Morphology Using blur, thresholding, erosion, dilation, and open/close operations to make target structure clearer.
04 · Understanding Shape: Contours and Geometry Turning image regions into describable objects through contours, centers, areas, and rotated boxes.
05 · Straightening the View: ROI Correction and Normalization Applying perspective transforms to standardize target regions for more stable downstream analysis.
06 · Entering Time: Multi-Object Handling and State Flow Moving from single-frame results to continuous trajectories and building a stronger sense of temporal logic.
07 · Next Route: More Reliable Vision Prototypes Continuing toward robustness, parameter reasoning, trajectory consistency, and cleaner engineering structure.
